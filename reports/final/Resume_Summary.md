# Transit Restoration Optimization Project (Seattle)

- Designed and implemented an end-to-end Python pipeline that fused GTFS archives, ACS demographics, and NOAA climate data to quantify service drift and accessibility inequities across King County Metro tracts.
- Engineered a schedule-based connectivity model with weather-adjusted travel friction, then translated historical service gaps into a 3,655-option restoration candidate library for optimization.
- Formulated and solved equity-aware and robust mixed-integer programs that optimized restoration portfolios under service-hour budgets, exposing measurable efficiency-equity tradeoffs and weather sensitivity.
- Produced publication-ready diagnostics and decision visuals (drift, volatility, fairness frontier, candidate map, robustness comparison) to support transparent planning recommendations for transit agencies.
